About me

My name is Sam and I am the owner of Dynamo Dog Walking.

I am a very keen animal lover that has lots of animal care qualifications, ranging from small animals through to farm animals. I also worked in Grays Kennels in Cumbria, where I realised I wanted to work with dogs every day!

What we do

Dog walking£8, 30mins+

This is the perfect choice for dogs who prefer to be walked alone. This is half an hour of walking which can be tracked via our app and a tracker on the collar and timed, and a quick wash if they’re muddy so your pet returns home looking and smelling as fresh as when they set off.

Drop-in visits£8, 30mins+

This visit can include various things from playing with the young energetic dogs, to a snooze and a stroke for the more relaxed dogs. I can also feed and administer medicine and I am happy to brush your dogs to help with any matting and messy hair.

Group dog walks£12, 1hr+

This walk is perfect for high energy dogs. This walk will be in a park or a safe place and dogs will be allowed off the lead (with permission) to have a run and a play. This walk will also include a quick wash at the end if they’re muddy to make sure your dog returns looking and smelling as good as when they left.


Watch a walk

Safety is key for me so every walk is GPS tracked with a tracker on your dog and on me via an app that sends you a text when we leave and when we get back home. Whether your dog can go off lead or not, they will get a full walk and play. Finally, every dog gets a quick wash if needed so they return home smelling fresh!

I'm DBS checked and available for walks in Wimblington, Chatteris, Doddington, March and wider Cambridgeshire.

Meet my dog, Coco

Let me introduce you to Coco!

She's a 10 year old rescue Rottweiler that's a bit nervous of everything! We've had her around 6/7 months now and she has come on leaps and bounds from being very defensive to now letting strangers pet her. Unfortunately, in that time we have had to remove a toe due to cancer.

If you ever see us out on a walk she'll definitely bark but please do say hello! She'll be wearing a muzzle just in case but she really just wants a stroke! Her favourite way to greet people is by pushing her head into your leg to make sure she gets all of your attention!

Unfortunately she wasn't socialized when she was younger so she's very barky at everyone and everything that moves but if the barking doesn't scare you she'll happily accept your cuddles!
